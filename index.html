What is TMS?

The foundation

Understanding the diagnosis Dr. Sarno spent his life sharing.

Tension Myositis Syndrome is a psychophysiological condition — meaning it originates in the mind and produces real, physical symptoms in the body. The pain is genuine. The suffering is genuine. But its source is not a herniated disc or structural damage. It is the brain deliberately reducing blood flow to muscles, nerves, or tendons to create physical pain as a distraction.

Dr. Sarno — a physiatrist at NYU Medical Center for over 30 years — noticed that the vast majority of his patients with back pain, neck pain, and repetitive stress injuries had one thing in common: they were people who put enormous pressure on themselves, who repressed difficult emotions like rage, fear, or grief.

The brain generates pain to keep those feelings unconscious. It is a protective mechanism that outlives its usefulness. Once you understand what your brain is doing — and why — the pain loses its purpose and can begin to fade.

"
The purpose of the pain is to keep your attention focused on the body, so that you will not become aware of the emotional state that exists in your unconscious mind.

— Dr. John E. Sarno

Key Principles

Core concepts

The three pillars of Sarno's healing framework.

01

The Emotional Origin

Your unconscious mind harbors emotions — rage, fear, sadness, shame — that it deems too threatening to allow into consciousness. The brain creates pain to keep you focused elsewhere.

02

Knowledge is the Cure

Unlike most conditions, TMS heals through understanding. When you recognize what your brain is doing and why, the strategy stops working. The pain loses its purpose and diminishes.

03

Resume Living Fully

Sarno urged patients to stop all physical treatments and return to full activity immediately. Treating TMS like a physical injury reinforces the brain's deception and prolongs symptoms.

Common Questions

Doubts welcomed
Research shows that herniated discs and other structural findings are extremely common in people with zero pain. Studies have found disc abnormalities in the majority of pain-free adults over 40. Sarno argued these findings are incidental — the actual cause of pain is the brain's oxygen deprivation strategy, which switches locations when you focus on a newly discovered "reason."
The autonomic nervous system controls blood flow throughout the body. When the brain decides to restrict oxygen to a region — muscles, tendons, nerves — it produces genuine, intense pain. There is nothing imaginary about it. The mechanism is physical; the trigger is psychological. We accept that stress causes headaches and stomach ulcers; TMS is the same principle applied to the musculoskeletal system.
The medical establishment is trained to look for — and treat — structural causes of pain. TMS requires no surgery, no medication, no ongoing treatment. It challenges deeply held assumptions about how pain works. Many physicians who initially dismissed Sarno have come around after seeing patients recover. The number of TMS-literate practitioners is growing steadily.
